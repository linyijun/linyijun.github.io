About Me

I am now seeking faculty and postdoctoral positions. Please feel free to reach out.

Currently, I am working with Prof. Yao-Yi Chiang in the Knowledge Computing Lab at the University of Minnesota (UMN) Department of Computer Science & Engineering.

Before that, I studied at the University of Southern California (USC), advised by Prof. Yao-Yi Chiang and Prof. José Luis Ambite.

My research lies in the areas of AI, machine learning, and spatial science. The overarching goal of my work is to understand how human-environment interactions evolve across space and time. A major focus of my work is on building machine learning approaches that embed spatial principles into data-driven models to generate reliable predictions and extract useful knowledge from sparse, heterogeneous, and multimodal data. My research is highly interdisciplinary, combining insights from computer science and domain expertise to solve challenging real-world problems with high societal impact.

Specific topics include:

  • Spatial prediction from sparse and irregular point observations
  • Spatiotemporal forecasting
  • Geospatial knowledge extraction from heterogeneous spatial data

Collaboration domains: environmental science, geospatial science, soil science, public health, physics and remote sensing, digital humanities, and library and information science.

More information is available in my CV and on our Lab website.
